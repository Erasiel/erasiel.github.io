About Me

I am a PhD student in the Department of Artificial Intelligence at the University of Szeged, advised by Prof. Márk Jelasity. My research spans the areas of AI safety, interpretability, and benchmarking.

My current works focus on fair, unbiased, and adaptive worst-case evaluations of generative models, representational interpretability and similarity, and generative model watermarking.

Before my PhD, I completed an MSc in Computer Science and a BSc in Business Informatics Engineering at the University of Szeged. My experience includes AI development at Bosch Hungary Ltd., research in multimodal medical image processing, and a 2025 research visit at NTU Singapore's Generative AI Lab, where I worked on world model safety.

Research Interests

Thesis Supervision

I actively supervise Bachelor's and Master's thesis students and welcome motivated students to work with me on problems within my research areas. If you are interested in writing your thesis in one of my research areas, please get in touch with a brief description of your background and motivation.

Students with exceptional results are recommended to take part in the Scientific Students' Associations Conference (in Hungarian: TDK) and publish their results in international conferences or journals.

So far, I have supervised:

  • 1 paper published at the CVPR 2026 workshops.
  • 1 TDK paper that was awarded 1st prize and an industrial special prize.
  • 1 MSc thesis.
  • 3 BSc theses.
