About Me

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Example, advised by Prof. John Smith. My research sits at the intersection of machine learning, natural language processing, and computational social science.

I am broadly interested in building robust AI systems that can reason about language in complex, real-world settings. My current work focuses on developing methods for low-resource NLP and evaluating the factuality of large language models.

Before starting my PhD, I received a B.Sc. in Mathematics and Computer Science from Example College (2021) and spent a year as a research assistant at the Institute of Advanced Computing. I have also interned at Company A and Company B.
